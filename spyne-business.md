# Spyne Business Overview

## Company Snapshot
Spyne is building an AI-native operating stack for automotive dealerships in the US.  
The company started with visual merchandising tools and is expanding into conversational AI agents for dealership sales and service workflows.

## Problem
US dealerships manage high-value inventory with high holding costs.  
They depend on fragmented software systems (DMS, CRM, inventory tools, service tools) and labor-heavy contact center operations, which creates:
- slow lead response and missed opportunities
- disconnected workflows across sourcing, merchandising, sales, and service
- high staffing cost and turnover in BDC/contact center roles

## Solution
Spyne's product strategy has two active pillars:

1. **Merchandising Platform (Studio)**
   - helps dealerships create high-quality vehicle media (images/360/video)
   - accelerates listing readiness and online publishing
   - improves conversion through better visual presentation

2. **Conversational AI Agents (Vinny)**
   - voice/SMS/email agents for inbound and outbound dealership workflows
   - supports lead qualification, follow-up, booking appointments, and service interactions
   - designed to operate continuously and consistently across channels

Long term, Spyne aims to connect these functions into a unified dealer operating system across:
**acquisition -> merchandising -> publishing -> sales -> service -> reacquisition**

## Traction (as discussed)
- Significant dealership footprint built through merchandising (1,500+ rooftops referenced)
- Reported expansion from low merchandising ACVs to higher blended ACVs with agent deployment
- Claimed strong recent monthly new bookings momentum
- Active deployment with large dealer groups and growing land-and-expand motion

## Business Model
Current monetization is a mix of fixed subscription-like pricing and dealer-size-based contracts.  
The stated direction is outcome-oriented pricing tied to sales/service performance metrics (appointments, show rates, conversions), positioned as a lower-cost alternative to human BDC staffing.

## Go-To-Market
- Initial wedge: merchandising product adoption across dealerships
- Expansion: upsell AI agent products into existing rooftops
- GTM execution combines inside sales and dealer-focused outreach with category-specific demos/use cases

## Competitive Positioning
Spyne positions itself as:
- vertically focused on automotive dealership workflows
- integration-heavy across legacy dealer systems
- differentiated by combining visual merchandising + conversational AI in one platform roadmap

## Near-Term Priorities
- scale sales inbound/outbound and service inbound agent deployments
- improve onboarding-to-live conversion from bookings
- deepen customer expansion across existing rooftops
- continue building toward AI-native CRM/inventory-adjacent capabilities

## Key Risks / Diligence Areas
- Gap between contracted bookings and fully live revenue
- Dependence on integrations with legacy dealership systems
- Competitive pressure from new AI-first entrants and incumbents
- Execution risk in scaling multiple agent lines while maintaining quality and ROI proof

## One-Line Thesis
Spyne is evolving from automotive merchandising software into an AI-native dealership operations platform, using conversational agents as the primary expansion engine.
