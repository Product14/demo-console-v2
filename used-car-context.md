# Used Car Playbook Context (Detailed Operating Reference)

This document is a detailed, software-ready conversion of the used-car playbook context into practical operating standards.

## Core Thesis

- `Speed` creates gross.
- `Structure` protects gross.
- `Discipline` compounds gross into net.

Primary management belief: most stores do not have a traffic problem, they have a consistency problem across buying, recon, pricing, conversion, and accountability.

## Strategic Intent

The used-car department should behave like a production system that turns:

- market intelligence into purchase decisions
- inventory into frontline-ready units in less than 3 days
- leads into shows and sold units through strict process
- front-end and back-end gross into strong net-to-gross and ROS
- manager standards into repeatable team behavior

## Benchmark Standard (Scoreboard Targets)

- Turn Rate: `1.6x-2.0x` monthly
- Total PVR: `$3,500+`
- Front-End Gross (after pack): `$1,500` per unit
- Back-End PVR: `$1,500-$2,000+` (goal: `$2,000+`)
- Net-to-Gross: `30%+`
- ROS: `4.5%+`
- Holding Cost: `$40-$50` per car/day
- Recon to Frontline-Live: `<3 days`
- Finance Penetration: `70%-75%`
- Service Contract Penetration: `45%-50%`
- 45+ Day Inventory: `<10%`
- Team Retention: `80%+` (leadership health metric)
- Training Compliance: `90%+` (leadership execution metric)

## Core Formulas (With Practical Use)

- `Market Day Supply (MDS) = (Total Market Inventory / 30-Day Retail Sales) x 30`
  - Use to prioritize segments with demand/supply imbalance.
- `Cost-to-Market % = (Your Price / Market Average Price) x 100`
  - Typical strategy window: `97%-99%`, adjusted by segment and age.
- `Gross ROI / Day = Front Gross / Days in Stock`
  - Use to determine hold vs markdown vs wholesale decision.
- `Holding Cost / Day`
  - Method: `(YTD used expense - YTD variable expense) -> / months -> / avg inventory -> / open days`.
- `Finance Penetration % = (Financed Deals / Total Retail Sales) x 100`
- `45+ Day Inventory % = (Aged Units / Total Inventory) x 100`
- `Net-to-Gross % = (Net Profit / Total Gross) x 100`
- `ROS = (Net Profit / Sales Revenue) x 100`
- `Cost per Sale = Ad Spend / Units Sold from that source`
- `Under Allowance = Appraised Value - Shown Value`
- `Potential Under Allowance Gross = Avg Under Allowance x Units Sold`

## Operating Commandments (Non-Negotiables)

- Buy from market demand and turn data, not manager preference.
- Approve recon ROs same day, no carry-over queue.
- Protect day 0-3 workflow to online live status.
- Track and eliminate invisible inventory (in stock but not live).
- Price by market rank, age, and gross plan; do not panic-chase.
- Hold line in first 15 days unless visibility data proves action needed.
- Execute mandatory 15-day and 45-day inventory walks.
- Require TO discipline in all channels (internet, phone, walk-in).
- Keep scoreboards visible at store, manager, and rep levels.
- Tie pay plans to the behaviors that produce profitable velocity.

## Chapter-Level Operating Logic

### 1) Market Roadmap (How to Win Market Share)

- Build a live market map by segment, price band, and competitor behavior.
- Pull 30-day sales volume and compare to owned inventory mix.
- Identify under-owned segments where demand exceeds current inventory position.
- Decision output each day: what to buy, what to avoid, where to reallocate.

### 2) Marketing Domination (Lead Quality Over Spend Volume)

- Marketing objective is profitable conversion, not vanity traffic.
- Structure channels by full funnel visibility: spend -> leads -> appointments -> sold.
- Frame messaging around buyer pain points (payment, trust, trade certainty).
- Own local ZIP-level market before broad conquest expansion.

### 3) Buying Strategy (Inventory That Moves)

- Start with MDS and retail velocity by segment.
- Evaluate every buy on three checks:
  - cost-to-market opportunity
  - recon burden and expected ROI
  - expected turn window
- Preferred source hierarchy: trades/service drive/off-lease/local overflows, then auctions with strict discipline.

### 4) Speed to Market (The Silent Margin Multiplier)

- Day 0: intake, appraisal, photos, RO generated.
- Day 1: mechanical completed.
- Day 2: cosmetic, detail, QC completed.
- Day 3: merchandising, pricing, and full online publication completed.
- Every delayed day burns holding cost and reduces final net.

### 5) Pricing Strategy (Data + Gross Discipline)

- Pricing triangle: market rank + cost-to-market + age.
- Age-based model:
  - Days 0-15: hold and optimize visibility/merchandising.
  - Days 16-30: controlled `1%-2%` action when justified.
  - Days 31-45: controlled `3%-5%` action when justified.
  - 45+: retail with hard objective, or wholesale disposition.
- Track rank zones: top 5 = high visibility, 6-10 = watch, 10+ = invisible risk.

### 6) Holding Cost (True Cost of Delay)

- Carrying cost includes interest, depreciation, payroll burden, and opportunity cost.
- Treat holding cost as daily tax against each unit.
- Use break-even day logic to force objective aging decisions.

### 7) 15-Day and 45-Day Walks (Inventory Pulse Checks)

- 15-day walk = preventive intervention:
  - photos, comments, market rank, physical condition, display quality.
- 45-day walk = decision point:
  - retail re-commit, aggressive price action, or wholesale exit.
- Assign explicit owner and due date per aged unit action.

### 8) Recon Cost and Door-Rate Discipline

- Undercharging internal labor hides true economics and weakens decisions.
- Bill internal work at realistic door-rate standards.
- Do not close RO until unit is live online and QC approved.

### 9) Internet / Phone / Walk-In Conversion

- Typical conversion windows:
  - internet: `8%-12%`
  - phone: `25%-35%`
  - walk-in: `35%-45%`
- First 15 minutes on internet lead response is critical.
- Use two-touch response pattern immediately (call + text/email).
- TO every serious shopper, including "just looking" traffic.

### 10) Accountability Systems

- Build accountability pyramid:
  - store-level outcomes (net, ROS, volume)
  - manager-level process KPIs (speed, age, response, TO)
  - rep-level activity metrics (calls, contacts, appointments, shows)
- Diagnose failure by broken funnel link, not by broad blame.

### 11) Pay Plans That Shape Behavior

- Pay design rules:
  - simple enough to understand
  - tied to controllable actions
  - aligned to speed + gross + process quality
- Avoid volume-only plans that erode gross discipline.

### 12) Packs and Margin Control

- Pack is legitimate if transparent, consistent, and tied to real cost structure.
- Formula anchor:
  - `(Fixed used expense + variable used overhead + recon cushion) / avg monthly sales`.
- Do not use inflated pack to hide process failures.

### 13) Under Allowance Strategy

- Controlled tool, not a deception model.
- Guardrails:
  - appraisals must be data-supported
  - controlled range by segment
  - consistent logging and manager oversight
  - use as negotiation flexibility, not margin fabrication

### 14) Finance Margin and Penetration

- Back-end is profit depth, not optional add-on.
- Focus levers:
  - lender mix
  - menu execution
  - product penetration by category
  - clean desk-to-F&I handoff
- Weekly check: penetration, PVR, chargeback, funded vs pending.

### 15) Profitability Metrics and Benchmarks

- Five pillars:
  - volume
  - gross
  - expense control
  - net-to-gross
  - ROS
- Use benchmark ladder (elite/strong/average/weak) to maintain urgency.

### 16) Standard That Wins (Conclusion)

- No silver bullet replaces operating discipline.
- Process consistency beats short-term heroics.

### 17) Leadership and Team Development

- Scale requires building bench strength.
- Train in live reps: appraisals, lot walks, TOs, value presentation.
- Measure leadership output: retention, internal promotions, compliance.

## Daily Management Cadence (Suggested Rhythm)

### Morning (30-45 minutes)

- Prior-day solds/gross by segment and source.
- Top units by rank-risk and age-risk.
- Recon queue review; approve every pending RO.
- Internet response-time and missed-opportunity review.

### Midday (20-30 minutes)

- BDC funnel check: leads, appointments set, confirmations.
- Same-day buy approvals based on live market data.
- Bottleneck sweep: service, detail, photos, comments, pricing.

### End of Day (20-30 minutes)

- Units not yet live online and reason code per unit.
- Aged units approaching trigger days (15, 30, 45).
- Tomorrow appointments and manager TO assignments.

## Weekly Leadership Rituals

### Monday Profit Review (30 minutes)

- Week total: sold, gross, net, net-to-gross, ROS.
- Top 5 and bottom 5 units by ROI/day.
- Recon speed and invisible inventory trend.
- F&I penetration/PVR and chargeback watchlist.
- Three corrective actions with owner and deadline.

### Inventory Walk Cadence

- 15-day walk (preventive): quality, visibility, pricing, presentation.
- 45-day walk (decision): retail reset vs wholesale release.

### Team Execution Rhythm

- Weekly chapter drill (30 minutes): one rule, one gap, one fix.
- Saturday huddle (under 10 minutes): process wins + daily focus.

## Diagnostic Trees (When KPI Misses Target)

### If turn rate is low

- Check inventory mix vs market demand first.
- Check recon-to-live time second.
- Check rank visibility and photo quality third.
- Check appointment show rates and TO discipline fourth.

### If front gross is collapsing

- Audit buying quality and recon spend accuracy.
- Audit pricing discipline (over-discounting early).
- Audit desk structure and under-allowance consistency.
- Audit aged-unit panic discount behavior.

### If net-to-gross is weak

- Validate holding cost assumptions are current.
- Check F&I penetration and product mix.
- Audit internal labor/door-rate leakage.
- Check advertising cost per sale by channel.

### If 45+ inventory is rising

- Verify 15-day walk compliance.
- Trigger mandatory action plans at day 30.
- Set weekly wholesale release quotas if needed.
- Stop buying segments already over-aged.

## Guardrails and Risk Controls

- No emotional auction purchases outside buy-box criteria.
- No RO aging without owner and reason code.
- No "ready" car considered complete until fully live online.
- No repricing without rank + age + ROI/day rationale.
- No invisible changes to pack or comp rules midstream.
- No under-allowance usage without logged appraisal support.

## 30/60/90 Day Rollout Plan

### Days 1-30 (Stabilize Visibility and Speed)

- Implement recon stage tracker and same-day RO policy.
- Publish daily visible scoreboard for speed, age, and response time.
- Start mandatory 15-day walk cadence.
- Baseline holding cost/day and current 45+ %.

### Days 31-60 (Discipline Pricing and Conversion)

- Launch age-bucket pricing rules and rank thresholds.
- Standardize internet/phone response and TO process.
- Enforce weekly funnel diagnosis (lead -> sold).
- Begin monthly benchmark-ladder review.

### Days 61-90 (Scale Profitability and Leadership)

- Align pay plans with speed/gross/process KPIs.
- Strengthen F&I handoff and product penetration management.
- Stand up leadership bench coaching and compliance tracking.
- Audit net-to-gross and ROS trend against target.

## One-Paragraph Executive Summary

This playbook runs a used-car department as an operating system: buy to market demand, get units frontline-live in under three days, price with rank/age/cost discipline, quantify daily holding cost, attack aging inventory with 15-day and 45-day walks, convert all lead channels with strict TO and response standards, align desk and F&I for total PVR depth, and manage everything through visible scoreboards, fixed review rhythms, and KPI-aligned compensation. The outcome is predictable gross, healthier net-to-gross, stronger ROS, and less dependence on heroic individuals.
